The Sport of Running

Running is a sport that allows you to go out and experience nature. It can be a therapeutic experience as it allows you to focus only upon running the miles that you are desiring to. While it is challenging, it can be very therapeutic and overall worth the pain.

If you are planning on becoming a beginner runner, here are a few tips!

  1. Proper Form and Technique
    • Maintain a relaxed posture
    • Lightly land on your midfoot and roll forward to push off your toes
    • Avoid overstriding; your food should land beneath your body
    • Swing your arms naturally, with elbows bent at aobut 90 degrees to maintain rhythm
  2. Appropriate Gear
    • Shoes: Wear running shoes that fit foot type and where you will run. Replace them every 300-500 miles.
    • Clothing: Dress appropriately for the weather and try to choose moisture-wicking fabrics.
    • Accessories: Consider safety gear or hydration packs, if needed.
  3. Gradual Progression and Recovery
    • Warm up and cool down to help prepare muscles for run and to aid in recovery.
    • Increase gradually to avoid sudden increases in mileage or intensity.
    • Rest and Recover by scheduling days to allow your body to rest to reduce the risk of injuries.
